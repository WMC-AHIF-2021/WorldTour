  • Kyrgyzstan
    The Central Asian nation of Kyrgyzstan is a landlocked mountainous country with a population of around 5 million. The ethnic Kyrgyz comprise 73.2% of the country’s population. Uzbeks and Russians are the largest minority ethnic groups accounting for around 14.6% and 5.8% of the total population, respectively.

  • Tash Rabat Caravanserai
    A major stop on the Silk Road, the 15th century Tash Rabat Caravanserai is almost an afterthought nowadays, with few tourists making it to the spectacular surroundings. The well-preserved ruins overlook the Tian Shan Mountains and if you’re based in Osh, a local tour guide will know the best way to get to this atmospheric landmark.

Uzbekistan

Uzbekistan is a double-landlocked country in Central Asia. It is surrounded by five landlocked countries: Kazakhstan to the north, Kyrgyzstan to the northeast, Tajikistan to the southeast, Afghanistan to the south and Turkmenistan to the south-west.

Kazakhstan

Kazakhstan, officially the Republic of Kazakhstan, is a country located mainly in Central Asia. It borders Russia in the north and west, China in the east, and Kyrgyzstan, Uzbekistan, and Turkmenistan in the south.

China

China is a country in East Asia. It is the world's most populous country, with a population of more than 1.4 billion. China spans five geographical time zones and borders 14 different countries, the second most of any country in the world after Russia.

Tajikistan

Tajikistan, officially the Republic of Tajikistan, is a landlocked country in Central Asia. It is bordered by Afghanistan to the south, Uzbekistan to the west, Kyrgyzstan to the north and China to the east.
