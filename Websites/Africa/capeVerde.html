    The gastronomy of Cape Verde is thus a mix between Africa and Portugal (are also called pasteis con peixe). It is mainly based on seafood such as lobster, prawn, and octopus but also chicken, pork, tuna and swordfish.   • Cap Verdes Culture


    Over 93% of the population of Cape Verde are Catholic Christians, and 5% are Protestants. The rest of the population are either adherents of other Christian denominations or are affiliated to Islam and the Bahá'í faith. Non-believers are rare in Cape Verde. Since Christianity is the dominant religion in Cape Verde, Christian festivals are celebrated with great festivities in the country.

  • Special Sights


    One of the biggest draws to Cape Verde is the spectacular scenery and diverse landscapes strewn around the islets. The laid-back fusion of African and Portuguese culture, perfect climate and endless outdoor pursuits from windsurfing to hiking also lures visitors. Cape Verde is world-renowned for its spectacular waves, and as a result, attracts professional and amateur water sport enthusiasts year-round.
